## 0. The operating rule (everything else serves this)

> Contact a firm only when ALL FIVE hold: strong FIT + a CURRENT DATED EVENT + a NAMED accountable person + a LEGITIMATE route to them + one DEFENSIBLE gap. If any is missing: nurture or hold. Never manufacture urgency.

This rule exists because the common failure of batches 1-2 was treating an account-level public fact (awards, a logo gap) as if it proved a person-level buying moment. It does not.

## 2. Fit, trigger, proof gap — three different things, never mixed

| Field | What it means | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| FIT | The account plausibly has recurring evidence work | multiple panels, many agencies, frequent awards, bid-coordinator role exists |
| TRIGGER | A dated event plausibly changed the workload NOW | actual panel refresh/application window, bid-role hire, accreditation renewal, acquisition, new service line |
| PROOF GAP | A specific public record can be checked and named | passed panel date, cert not confirmable on a register, stale capability PDF |

Fit qualifies an account. A trigger times the touch. A proof gap earns the first sentence. Multi-panel membership is FIT, not timing — Griffin Legal's 12 panels make it a strong account, and still ordinary cold outreach without a current event.

## 3. Niches (recast honestly)

### Hypothesis H1 — Newly-onboarded panel suppliers (UNVALIDATED — test, don't assume)
Theory: joining a panel converts occasional bidding into on-demand submission-readiness. Panel refreshes publish dated new-supplier lists (DTA/BuyICT/GovCMS announcements, AusTender SON supplier lists), so the event is public and dated.
Honesty check from our own records: ~11 of 46 researched firms (~24%) were confirmed panel/standing-offer holders — but ZERO were verified as newly added with a recent join date. We have proven panel membership exists in the market, not that a weekly stream of fresh joiners exists, nor that joining creates an immediate unmanaged evidence problem (eligibility ≠ invitations; some joiners have mature bid ops). Validation gate: find 5 genuinely new suppliers in one month of watching; send 5 snapshot notes; review before declaring this a niche.

### Niche D — Defence/assurance-heavy SMEs (DEPTH PLAY, best gap quality)
Firms publicly self-declaring DISP membership and/or ISO/NATA/SOC 2/AS9100 stacks operate in the most evidence-scrutinised regimes. No public DISP/JOSCAR register exists (confirmed) — the signal is their own self-declaration, checkable against public registers (JAS-ANZ for ISO; NATA for lab scope). Qualify the claim: requirements vary by prime, work package and classification — self-declaration signals assurance exposure, not that every standard is "required to be considered."
From our own records this niche is real: RD Consulting (DISP + JOSCAR + multiple panels), Work Science (NATA + ISO 45001), Testgrid (ISO 27001 + SOC 2), Australian Survey Research (ISO 20252), Next Peak (ISO x3 + Defence work).

### Enrichment — multi-panel footprint (FIT only)
AusTender SONs + the data.gov.au bulk dataset give a per-firm panel footprint. Use to RANK accounts, never to justify a touch by itself. Panel expiry is a verification prompt, not proof of a live requalification burden (arrangements get extended, refreshed in tranches, or closed without supplier action — Interaction Consulting's passed CSIRO date is the canonical "check, don't assert" example).

Down-weighted: modern slavery (A$100M threshold — flow-down talking point only); NDIS (audit reports not public); aged care (public reports — parked future variant).

## 4. Trigger catalogue (with the ones Codex's field research added)

Dated events worth a touch (subject to the §0 rule):
- Actual panel refresh / application window / new-supplier publication (H1)
- Accreditation events: NATA scope change, RTO registration, SOC 2 report period, Supply Nation status, JOSCAR renewal
- Bid/tender coordinator hired, departed, or being recruited (SEEK/Indeed) — two-sided signal: proves resourced bid load AND may mean they're solving it internally; use as fit+trigger, expect some "we have it covered"
- Acquisition, merger, rapid office expansion (real consolidation pain for claims/CVs/case studies)
- New service line or new government supplier profile published
- Capability statement / price book newly published, materially updated, or visibly stale
- A public panel/cert date already passed — phrase as a check, never as proof of lapse
- Cross-agency award surge — only in combination with a hire or profile update

Detection caveats (unchanged, honest): AusTender SON pages 403 automated fetches (semi-manual via UI/bulk data). JAS-ANZ register confirmed from documentation only — a live lookup procedure with entity-name variations and screenshot record MUST be proven before the first snapshot ships. A register miss can be an entity-name/certifier/scope mismatch: always "could not confirm," never "you have no valid certificate."

## 5. The first touch: plain-text note, no attachment

The snapshot survives, but its DELIVERY changes based on execution experience:
- PLAIN TEXT email to a VERIFIED NAMED work address. One source link. NO PDF on first touch (attachments from unknown senders create trust and filtering friction; our Gmail attachment mechanics also proved fragile — duplicates, unreadable draft IDs, no in-place editing). The PDF is sent after a reply or on request.
- Named-recipient gate: if the right person's work address cannot be verified, the firm goes to HOLD. A director's name on a page does not fix delivery to info@. Functional inboxes only when they are the firm's explicit tender/procurement route.
- The three-clause structure stands (trigger + on-site gap + register check), every clause externally verifiable and sourced by name:
> "You [dated event]. On your own site, ISO 9001 and 45001 appear as logos without certificate numbers or expiry dates, and I couldn't confirm them on the JAS-ANZ public register. When a buyer asks you to evidence those under time pressure, that scramble is what we remove."
- Bounded: observations only, never the fix. Accurate: one wrong observation is permanent trust loss; two-source rule before any claim.

## 6. Channels (conditional ranking, from execution experience)

1. Warm referral from a certifier, bid writer, panel adviser, or existing client — the highest-trust route; build a 10-name partner shortlist once a sample deliverable exists to show them.
2. Plain-text email to a verified named work address (the default cold route).
3. LinkedIn only after genuine shared context (event, referral, a post of theirs) — a connect from a stranger who has audited your certificates can feel invasive.
4. Functional inbox only when it is the firm's explicit tender/proposal route.
5. Cold PDF / generic info@ — last resort, effectively retired.

## 7. Offer ladder (with promises the service can keep)

1. FREE — the snapshot note (2-3 sourced observations, plain text).
2. LOW — "Evidence Gap Audit," fixed price sub-A$750: the snapshot done in depth, sourced register of what's missing/stale, no drafting. The smaller yes.
3. CORE — A$750 founding pilot (20 sourced cards + one drafted section + gap-and-expiry report). Unchanged.
4. RECURRING (EXPLICITLY UNVALIDATED) — evidence-currency monitoring. Honest promise: "we reduce the risk of stale evidence reaching a buyer" — NOT "zero expired-evidence mistakes" (a guarantee the service cannot make and the ops rules forbid). Do not sell until a paid pilot completes AND the monitoring sources prove reliable (several are semi-manual today).

## 8. Cadence (sized to Will's actual week)

Replace the daily-blocks fantasy with:
- One 90-minute TRIGGER SPRINT per fortnight: scan sources, verify at most TWO targets to full confidence (entity, size, event date, named recipient, gap checked against registers). Verification is the real cost — batch experience says a safe snapshot takes well over 15 minutes when identity/role/route checks are done properly.
- One 20-minute follow-up block per week: reply handling, follow-up dates from the prospect kits, ledger hygiene.
- Will reviews and sends everything. Drafts only, unchanged.
- REVIEW GATE: after 8 genuine triggered sends (verified delivered), stop and assess replies/routing/bounces before continuing. No scale decisions on fewer than that; a 15-send/20% rule would let 3 replies trigger scaling — too noisy.

## 9. Ledger and metrics (measure the funnel honestly)

- One authoritative prospect ledger across ALL cohorts (batches 1-2, snapshots, referrals) with per-row: fit score, trigger + date + source URL, named recipient + route, gap + sources, suppression status, and OUTCOME tracked separately as bounce / wrong-person / no-reply / reply / qualified pain / paid.
