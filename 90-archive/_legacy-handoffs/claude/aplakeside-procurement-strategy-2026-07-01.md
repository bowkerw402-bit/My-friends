_Claude session: 2026-07-01_

## 1. PROCUREMENT THRESHOLD NAVIGATION

All three packages (Standard $3,250 / Elite $4,950 / Premium $7,950) sit below the $10k Commonwealth direct-purchase threshold. EL1 can approve unilaterally — no purchase order, tender, or panel required.

**Language to give buyers for internal use:**
> "This sits under the agency direct-purchase threshold and can be approved at EL1 delegation — no procurement panel required."

**EL2 / SES Band 1:** EL2 typically holds $25k–$50k delegation. One EL2 = potential multiple team bookings.

**ACT Government:** ACTPS separate framework. Under $10k approved by Band 7/8 equivalent. Same pitch works.

**Consulting/Defence primes:** No procurement process under ~$15k–$25k. Team lead approves from L&D or team budget. Drop all acquittal/delegation framing — lead with ROI.

**Search strings:**
- APS EL1: `"EL1" "Australian Public Service" "Canberra"` — filter 2nd connections, 500+ connections
- ACT Gov: `"Band 7" OR "Band 8" OR "Senior Manager" "ACT Government" "Canberra"`
- Consulting: `"Manager" OR "Senior Manager" "Deloitte" OR "EY" OR "PwC" OR "KPMG" "Canberra"`
- Defence: `"Team Lead" OR "Program Manager" "BAE Systems" OR "Thales" OR "Boeing Defence" "Canberra"`

**Connect vs. skip:**
- Connect if: role matches, Canberra confirmed, profile shows team management
- Skip if: contractor, no Canberra, profile dormant 90+ days

**Weekly cadence:**
- Monday: 10–15 connection requests (personalised note)
- Thursday: follow up accepted connections from prior week
- Never send connection + pitch same day — wait 2–3 days after acceptance

**3-step warm-up (3x reply rate):**
1. Like/comment on one recent post before connecting
2. Connect with brief note referencing their work/agency
3. After acceptance, open with a question — not a pitch

## 3. COLD EMAIL ARCHITECTURE

**Subject line formulas:**

| Segment | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| APS EL1 | `[Agency] team day — [Month]` | `Home Affairs team day — August` |
| ACT Gov | `Half-day on the lake for your [directorate] team` | same |
| Consulting | `Team cohesion that actually sticks — Lake BG, Canberra` | same |
| Defence | `Off-site for [Company] Canberra teams — Lake Burley Griffin` | same |

**4-paragraph structure:**
1. Hook — specific to their agency/context
2. What it is — lake-based, half-day, up to 12 people
3. Why it works for them — segment-specific pain
4. CTA — soft ask: *"Happy to send through details if this is the right time of year for your team."*

**Send timing:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 7:45am AEST.

**5-touch follow-up cadence:**
- Day 0: Initial email
- Day 5: "Just checking this landed"
- Day 12: Value-add detail (dietary, logistics, flexibility)
- Day 21: Social proof (anon agency reference)
- Day 30: Final close + provisional date offer

After Day 30: quarterly reactivation. Peak booking months: February and August.

## 4. APSJOBS INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM

**Weekly workflow (15 min, Monday morning):**
1. APSJobs.gov.au → filter: Location = ACT, Level = EL1, Ongoing or Non-ongoing
2. Capture: Agency, Branch/Division, backfill vs. expansion
3. Log to CRM as warm target

**Signals:**
- Backfill = team intact, new EL1 onboarding → pitch at 4–8 weeks post-appointment
- Multiple EL1 postings same agency same month = restructure → wait 6 weeks
- EL2 posting = budget holder in transition → hold

**6-week post-close window:** Set reminder when role closes. New EL1 starts in 4–8 weeks. They want a win. A team day is low-risk, high-visibility for them.

## 5. IPAA ACT EVENT STRATEGY

**Positioning:** Not a sponsor. A known face. Avoid vendor energy.

**What to say when asked "what do you do":**
> "We run half-day team experiences on Lake Burley Griffin — on the water, structured around the ILS capability framework. We've had a few agencies out this year — it's a good way to give your team something genuinely different without the usual conference-room fatigue."

_(Swap "ILS" for "ACTPS framework" with ACT Gov audiences. Drop framework reference entirely for consulting.)_

**Conversion framework:**
- Event 1: Introduce to 8–10 people, say the line, collect names — do not pitch
- 48 hours: LinkedIn connect referencing the event
- 2 weeks: Email with "following up from [event]" subject
- Benchmark: 15–20% of event conversations → booked call within 60 days

**Corporate partner angle:** IPAA ACT membership = event invitations + speaking slots. Worth it at 6+ events/year.

## 6. SEGMENT MESSAGING VARIANTS

**APS EL1 (federal — conservative climate)**
- Lead with: team cohesion in a period of change
- Vocabulary: "off-site," "team day," "capability-aligned," "low-admin booking"
- Avoid: "investment," "ROI," "efficiency" (loaded in current APS climate)
- Core sentence: *"A structured half-day on the lake — builds team connection without the usual compliance overhead."*

**ACT Government**
- Lead with: ACTPS values (respect, integrity, collaboration)
- Vocabulary: "ACTPS framework," "directorate," "Branch"
- Core sentence: *"We work regularly with ACT Government teams — familiar with the ACTPS framework and what meaningful development looks like in your context."*

**Consulting (Deloitte/EY/PwC/KPMG/Accenture/Leidos)**
- Lead with: team performance, client delivery resilience, retention signal
- Vocabulary: "engagement," "cohesion," "retention," "client-ready teams"
- No mention of acquittal, procurement, or delegation
- Core sentence: *"Best-performing consulting teams in Canberra aren't the ones that do the most — they're the ones that recover fastest. We build that."*

**Defence primes (BAE/Boeing/Thales/Northrop)**
- Lead with: team trust under pressure, mission alignment
- Vocabulary: "mission-aligned," "program team," "operational cohesion"
- More formal tone — structured sign-off culture
- Core sentence: *"Designed for teams that operate in high-stakes environments — a day on the lake that builds the trust your program needs."*

## 7. REFERRAL PROGRAM MECHANICS

**Structure:** $250 account credit (not cash) per referred booking that converts. Applied to next booking.

**Three optimal ask moments:**
1. Post-booking confirmation: *"If any other team leaders in your network would benefit, we do offer a credit for referred bookings that go ahead."*
2. On the day, 30 min before wrap (energy is high): *"If this is something you'd recommend to a colleague, we'd love an introduction — we look after referrers."*
3. Day-3 follow-up email: closes with *"If you'd like to refer a colleague, reply to this email with their name and we'll handle the rest."*

**Tracking:** Add "Referrer" column to CRM. Ask "how did you hear about us?" at first contact. Log referrer name. Trigger credit manually on booking conversion.
