Drafted by Claude from a dedicated web-research pass; adversarially reviewed by Codex against the existing APLakeside operating documents (`Business/_ops/2026-07-13-codex-strategy-review.md`); this version incorporates every MUST finding — including a full retraction of v1.0's procurement-threshold framing. ## 0. The operating rule

> Contact only when ALL FIVE hold: strong account FIT + a CURRENT dated team-level event (≤120 days) + a NAMED accountable person + a LEGITIMATE route to them + a defensible public warrant for the approach. If any is missing: nurture or hold. Never manufacture urgency.

Account-level facts (a census plan, an old MoG change) never justify a personal approach by themselves. That was v1.0's central error.

## 1. Procurement language — the hard rules (v1.0 retraction)

v1.0 claimed the sub-A$10k price "keeps a defensible spend out of procurement-scrutiny territory" and told Will to say an EL1 can approve unilaterally. **Both claims are withdrawn and banned.** They contradict the existing outreach pack, and "avoiding scrutiny" framing reads as advising a buyer to structure spend — commercially and reputationally dangerous in public-sector sales.

What we may truthfully say:
- The packages are modest, clearly scoped, and come with a quote, service agreement, insurance details and delivery records "to support assessment through your agency's normal process."
- NOT: that a threshold equals a delegation; that no procurement process applies; that the spend is pre-acquittable; that paperwork reclassifies the day.

Substance over vocabulary: renaming an offsite a "capability day" defends nothing if the substance looks like hospitality. The defensible core is a real agenda with named learning/operating objectives, qualified facilitation, tangible team outputs, proportionate catering, and a clear business reason. Materials must show that substance — the words follow.

Language rules (unchanged from v1.0, still right): never "retreat," "away-day," "team-building fun," "reward," "celebration." Use "structured capability day," "team transition support," "facilitated development session." Never imply a team is dysfunctional. Only cite public documents, never inferred private facts.

Artefacts: the Capability Development Record and attendance register are delivery evidence, clearly labelled NON-ACCREDITED. No per-attendee "certificates" (risks implying an accredited credential). Never claim the record is "reportable against" an agency's census plan — only the agency's action owner can decide what counts toward their plan. We provide an internal-justification TEMPLATE the buyer adapts and approves themselves.

## 2. Climate context (unchanged, still the frame)

APS austerity is live in mid-2026: VR rounds across many agencies, extra savings directives, ~A$2.7bn booked from reduced external spend (sources §10). Discretionary-looking spend is politically exposed; the substance-first framing above is the response. Redundancy handling: a live VR round is a HIGH-RISK REVIEW FLAG requiring current confirmation before any touch — not a blanket permanent ban (coverage lags; rounds can be function-specific). Re-approach 2-3 months post-round with transition framing, if at all.

## 3. Trigger hierarchy (recast: two-level, freshness-gated)

Ranked. Every trigger needs the §0 five gates; "second proof" means a team-level event within 120 days.

1. **Warm event or referral + verified team change** — a real IPAA/event conversation or a referral, where the person's team also has a current formation/merger/new-leader event. The only genuinely warm opener.
2. **Confirmed new EL1/EL2 + current team formation signal** — appointment confirmed by BOTH Gazette/APSJobs AND a current org/professional profile (a vacancy close is not an appointment). Honest caveats: new leaders may lack discretionary budget, avoid visible spend in their first 90 days, or inherit suppliers — this is a hypothesis with the highest individual-buyer intent, not a sure thing. HARD RULE: never contact the vacancy's listed contact officer unless independently verified as the relevant leader (they are usually recruitment admin).
3. **Census action plan + current team-level trigger + named owner** — the agency's published plan (ABS, Industry, PM&C, Ombudsman identified so far) is ACCOUNT CONTEXT: it warrants relevance, not a recipient. Actionable only when paired with (a) the published action owner where named, or the verified People/Capability/Workforce executive, or (b) a branch leader with their own ≤120-day event. The outreach line references their published commitment as context — it never tells them what counts toward it.
4. **Fresh MoG change + current named implementation team** — the May 2025 AAO is now ~14 months old: RETIRED as a live trigger unless current integration evidence exists (2026 implementation update, new structure, clustered hiring, new branch head). Future AAOs: check when announced (event-driven) or quarterly — not a weekly ritual. And a moved function ≠ a merged struggling team; corroborate before assuming.
5. **Agency-wide plan alone** → nurture/content audience only. **Old MoG alone** → reject.

Timing overlay (not triggers): EOFY L&D window (May-June) and February/August booking peaks. Never claim to "notice" underspend — it is not observable.

## 4. "Why THEM" lines (rewritten to survive the procurement rules)

T3 census (to a published action owner): "Your census action plan commits to [exact quoted action]. We run a facilitated capability day built around exactly that kind of team work — with a written development record of what was covered, and a draft internal-justification template your team can adapt through your normal assessment process."

T2 new leader (verified appointee, warm-ish route only): "Congratulations on the [role] move. If an early, structured session with your new team would be useful, we run facilitated capability days on the lake — clearly scoped, with a quote, agreement and delivery records ready for your agency's normal process."

T1 referral/event follow-up: reference the actual conversation; attach nothing; propose fifteen minutes.

## 5. Improve the buyer's end (offer moves, procurement-safe)

1. **Sample agenda, published**: objectives, facilitated exercises, outputs, follow-through — the substance document that makes the day defensible. Highest priority artefact.
2. **Internal-justification template**: explicitly "adapt and approve through your own process." We draft the buyer's upward-sell honestly instead of overclaiming.
3. **Capability Development Record productised** as a clearly non-accredited delivery record mapped to APS capability language (accurately — no framework compliance claims).
4. **Facilitation-first low-hospitality variant** (test, not pivot): a shorter paid planning/facilitation session without the boat component, for buyers where hospitality optics block the full package. Validates the relationship at lower political cost; upsell the full day later.
5. **New Leader's First 90 Days framing** for Standard/Elite — narrative only, same delivery, with the team-baseline leave-behind.

1. Genuine referral, or a real IPAA/event conversation (quarterly deliberate attendance — a BD activity, not a checklist row).
2. Named work email from the authenticated `williambowker@aplakeside.com.au` (Titan) sender — plain text, no unsolicited attachments. **Titan is the sending system for this venture; Gmail is staging/review only.** Maintain ONE send ledger across both so staged Gmail copies are never counted as sends and Titan sends are never duplicated.
3. LinkedIn only after real shared context or the recipient publicly discussed the trigger.
4. Official general route, asking who owns team capability/transition (an honest question, not a disguised pitch).
5. Cold email to recruitment contacts or broad executive lists: rejected.

## 7. First-ten target construction (method, not invented names)

| Slot | Account | Recipient type | Required second proof |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1-2 | PM&C | published action owner / named branch leader | exact action + current ownership / team event ≤120 days |
| 3-4 | ABS | same | same |

Rules: a slot stays EMPTY if no named person + legitimate route verifies — never backfill weak names to reach ten. Score each candidate on the §0 gates before drafting a word.

## 8. Cadence (sized to reality)

- ONE weekly 60-minute qualification block, capped at two contact-ready prospects. Covers: APSJobs sweep (T2 pipeline + 6-week reminders), trigger verification, drafting for Will's review.
- AAO check: event-driven/quarterly. Census plans: once per annual release (November sweep loads the February booking window; the four 2025 plans are processed NOW as the first batch).
- Redundancy review: confirm current status per-target during qualification (not a separately maintained monthly blacklist that goes stale).
- IPAA: quarterly, deliberate.
- Will reviews and sends everything.

## 9. Metrics

- Track separately: bounce / wrong-person / no-reply / reply / call / provisional booking / delivered day / referral. Wrong-person routing is its own failure mode, not "no reply."
- No reply-rate targets until Titan sending records (authentication, delivery, bounces) are reconciled — the same ledger-first discipline as the Evidence Desk.
- Review after 8 verified triggered sends; a 20-touch rule cannot distinguish trigger failure from routing/trust/seasonality problems.
