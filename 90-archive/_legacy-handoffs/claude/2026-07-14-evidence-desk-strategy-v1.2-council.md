# 10 — Client procurement plan (v1.2, council-corrected)

Doc control: v1.2 FINAL, 2026-07-14, owner Will. v1.0 Claude draft → v1.1 Codex adversarial review → v1.2 LLM Council verdict (5 advisors + 5 peer reviews; transcript summarised in the council verdict of 2026-07-14). **FREEZE CLAUSE: this document does not get rewritten again until 8 verified sends and 3 human conversations have happened. Strategy churn is the failure mode this version exists to stop.**

## Why v1.2 exists (plain words)

It has not been going well. 50 firms researched, ~33 messages prepared, zero replies, zero clients ever — and the honest diagnosis is not "bad targeting." It is: (1) we don't even know if the emails were delivered, (2) a proofless stranger can't clear this buyer's trust bar by cold email no matter how clever the trigger, and (3) the AI system made research and drafting so cheap that they impersonated progress. Talking to humans is the constraint. Everything below serves that.

## The sequence (do not reorder)

### Phase 0 — Falsify (one afternoon, before anything else)
1. Reconcile the send ledger: for every batch-1/2 recipient, confirm in the Sent folder whether it actually sent, and when. Anything unconfirmed is marked DEAD — no salvaging, no follow-ups on unconfirmed sends (cancel those clocks).
2. Deliverability seed test: send test notes to seed addresses (Gmail, Outlook, an M365 tenant); check spam placement; verify SPF/DKIM/DMARC on the sending identity.
3. Verdict: only after this do we know whether "0 replies" means anything. Status: reconciliation delegated to Codex (read-only Gmail pass) 2026-07-14.

### Phase 1 — Warm proof (this fortnight, parallel with Phase 0)
4. Three no-pitch discovery calls: bid writers, tender consultants, or a firm that recently lost a bid. Ask how they manage evidence today. If nobody describes the pain unprompted, the offer gets reshaped before more outreach.
5. One delivered artifact via a warm route (past employer, friend's firm, any personal connection) — free, contractually swapped for a named testimonial and redacted sample cards for the site.
6. The 14-day money lever: personally contact the 2 warmest already-researched firms (RD Consulting, Interaction Consulting) offering a free 30-minute evidence review. Skip the full verification ritual — they're already researched.
7. One hour of category proof: find anyone, anywhere, selling bid-evidence readiness. Competitors validate the category.
8. Market arithmetic: count the plausible qualifying pool (ACT + national). If ~50 firms was most of it, the ceiling — not cadence — is the next strategy question (but not before 8 sends).

### Phase 2 — Triggered cold outreach (only after Phase 0 verdict; third-priority channel behind warm and referral)
The v1.1 trigger doctrine survives here intact: contact only on [fit + current dated event ≤120 days + named person + legitimate route + defensible public gap]; fit/trigger/proof-gap are separate fields; "not visible publicly" banned as a gap except for publicly-verifiable items; "could not confirm" phrasing; two-source rule. ## The offer (corrected)

- **Language:** buyer words only. Not "evidence register/cards" — say "one document listing your 20 key certificates, insurances and claims, each with its source and expiry date, plus one drafted section of your next bid." Say what it physically is.
- **Reframe around the live bid:** the pitch is "I'll build the evidence pack for the bid you're writing right now" — the reusable document is the byproduct they keep.
- **Ladder collapsed:** free snapshot → A$750 pilot. No paid-audit rung until someone asks for a smaller step. Monitoring retainer stays parked until a pilot completes.
- **Client #1 special:** A$400-500, contractually exchanged for a named testimonial, case-study rights, and two introductions. Client #1 is a marketing asset priced accordingly.
- **Price in the first note:** "$750 fixed, delivered in five business days, you get X and Y" + a calendar link. No mystery meetings.

## The first touch (corrected)

- Give the ENTIRE finding away as a genuinely useful one-pager — "keep this, no strings." Sell the fix, not the diagnosis. (The old bounded-snapshot framing read as an audit threat.)
- Plain text, no attachment, to a verified named person. Signed by a real human: name, LinkedIn, one honest credential sentence (APS/procurement background). Photo and credential on the site.
- Evidence Desk gets its own one-page site and sending identity, separate from the multi-service storefront (a buyer who checks and finds lake days + websites + AI agents reads "will try anything"). Other storefront lines de-emphasised until one has revenue.

## Execution rules (corrected)

- **Weekly sprint (not fortnightly), with a FLOOR:** minimum 2 sends/week once Phase 0 clears. Sprint success = emails in the Sent folder. A 0-send sprint is logged as a failure with the blocking step named.
- **The JAS-ANZ check:** test live once; the moment it costs >15 minutes per lookup, downgrade to "cert claimed on site" and move on.
- **The scoreboard has one metric: human conversations held per week.** Drafts, research rows and strategy documents score zero. Minimum one phone call or in-person event per week — the email-only design was partly rejection-avoidance, and the council said so.
- **Agents' new job:** finding warm paths (shared connections, associations, events) — not more drafting.
- Review gate: after 8 verified sends AND 3 conversations, assess. Not before, not later.
