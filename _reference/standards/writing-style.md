# Writing style

## No dashes as punctuation (set 2026-07-18)
**The rule:** never use em dashes, en dashes, or a hyphen standing in for a pause or an aside. Not in chat
replies, emails, website copy, documents, reports, or anything else. Use a comma, a full stop, a colon, or
brackets instead.

**Will's words:** "I hate fucking hyphens. I don't want any of those in pretty much anything I do."

**Why it matters:** they read as machine written, and he finds them jarring.

**Practical:** ordinary hyphenated words that would break if you removed the hyphen (two-agent, follow-up,
one-off) are still normal English and are fine. It is the dash used as punctuation that is banned. If a
sentence feels like it wants a dash, split it into two sentences or use a comma.

**Applies to:** both agents, every surface, always.

## Plain language over jargon (set 2026-07-18)
Do not use internal shorthand with Will without explaining it. If a task name would not make sense to
someone who has not read the folder, say what it actually means in plain words first. He should never have
to ask "what does that mean" about his own work.
